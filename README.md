# MetamorphicSulfur
[![DOI](https://zenodo.org/badge/DOI/10.5281/zenodo.20169473.svg)](https://doi.org/10.5281/zenodo.20169473)

Data and code to reproduce the analyses in Stewart et al. (2026), Metamorphic sulfur release as a driver of sustained cooling and mass extinction, Science Advances

MATLAB scripts for generating metamorphic C and S fluxes
--

1) thermaldiffusion.m: Uses finite different to calculate a Pressure-Temperature-time history along a 1-D profile and outputs the following files for subsequent use:
- time 	- vector giving the age of each timestep in years	
- dtyrs	- the size of the timestep used in thermaldiffusion.m
- z	- vector giving depth values in meters
- dz	- the size of the spatial step (z) used in thermaldiffusion.m
- x	- vector indicating at each depths whether the rock type is sedimentary (1) or igneous (2)

2) degas2.m: Calculates a time series of S and CO2 flux to the atmosphere using the parameters generated by thermaldiffusion.m  Additional required input files include:
- Pm.mat	- a meshgrid of pressures from 1 to 1000 bar
- Tm.mat	- a mesh grid of temperatures from 200 to 1200 celsius
- Gtot.mat 	- the Gibbs Free Energy change of the sulfur releasing reaction, keyed to the Pm and Tm meshgrids (in Joules)
- GotC.mat	- the Gibbs Free Energy change of the carbon releasing reaction, keyed to the Pm and Tm meshgrids (in Joules)
- massSrem.mat	- the mass of sulfur remaining at equilibrium, keyed to the Pm and Tm meshgrids (in kg m-3)
- massCrem.mat	- the mass of carbon remaining at equilibrium keyed to the Pm and Tm meshgrids (in kg m-3)

3) degas2.m: Calls on Rxnrate_C.m and Rxnrate_S.m to calculate reaction kinetics for carbon and sulfur, respectively. The preferred kinetic parameters must be enabled in the Rxnrate scripts by uncommenting the desired model and commenting out other models. The scripts default to AR98 and HF97 for carbon and sulfur, respectively.

Python scripts for calculating climate impact of metamorphic C and S fluxes
--
SimpleClimateModel.ipynb Jupyter Notebook for converting metamorphic C and S fluxes into CO2 concentration (carbon cycle model) and effective radiative forcings (climate model emulator).

Data included:
--
TT_Xflux_MM.csv: Annual fluxes of X (C for carbon, S for sulfur) for a sill exmplacement of TT (Instantaneous or 10yr) using model MM (AR (default), eqm, LR, Ro for carbon; AR, eqm, Fegley, HF (default), Linert, LR for sulfur). For the volcanic-like emissions in Figure 9, use MM = AR_volceq for carbon and MM = HF_volceq for sulfur.
